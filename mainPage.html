Equatorial Eastern Pacific

The higher acidity in the east is primarily due to the ocean current patterns and sea surface temperature in the Pacific. The ocean current in the Eastern Pacific is an upwelling, which brings CO2 from the deep ocean to the surface, leading to an increased CO2 concentration in surface waters. Additionally, the cooler sea temperatures in the Eastern Pacific enhance the solubility of CO2, leading to an increase in acidity.

Black Sea

Such high level of acidification of the Black sea upper layer is mostly due not to the rise of carbon dioxide concentration in the atmosphere, but to natural quasi-periodical decadal-scale intensification of upward motions in the subsurface layer of the Black sea transporting the water with low-pH to the surface.

North Indian Ocean

The topography of India rises to the north, so all the pollutants (e.g. fertilisers) from the Himalayan region and the Indo-Gangetic plains concentrate and mix with the sea water from the Indo-Gangetic plains, which ultimately flows into the sea.
